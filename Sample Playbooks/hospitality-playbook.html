Welcome

Welcome to the Team

We're so excited to have you on the team! All of our teams are vital in making people feel welcome and comfortable at our church. Please take some time to review this booklet. The information listed here is designed to equip you to host with hospitality and excellence. Our hope is that your smile, friendly face and sincere personality can contribute to the life-giving atmosphere we desire every person to experience.

We lead more effectively when we know what is expected of us. Clarifying expectations reduces stress, brings focus to action and makes teams more productive. These expectations are laid out in three key areas: Expectations, Wins and Responsibilities.

Section 1

Expectations

Personal

Consistently seek personal growth and development:

  • Pursue a relationship with God strengthened by daily Bible reading and prayer.
  • Commit to attending one service and serving one service.
  • Commit to ongoing team training and actively participating in the life of the campus.

Team

Faithfully serve God's House and others:

  • Show up on time, with a great attitude and meet someone new.
  • Stay focused on guests when serving.
  • Pray for your team members, leaders and pastors.
  • Be open to building genuine relationships with others on the team.
Section 2

Wins

Every person works together to create an amazing guest experience. When one team member wins, the whole team wins! By accomplishing at least one of these things each weekend, you help the team win.

  • Engage in a Meaningful Connection with someone new each week. This could be a first-time guest or someone who has been coming for a while that you haven't met before.
  • Serve guests with a cheerful heart. Be present and friendly when guests start to arrive.
  • Meet 2–3 New People each week. They could be new to the church or just new to you.
Section 3

Responsibilities

The responsibilities outlined below clarify the "what" and "when" of each role and when executed will help create productive and healthy teams.

During the Week
  • Read weekly Team Leader communication regarding rosters, service flow, weekend elements and upcoming events.
  • Use Planning Center Online to confirm or decline your rostered position for the weekend. Communicate with your Team Leader if your schedule changes.
  • Connect with your Team Leader weekly for updates and any information you may need to make the weekend great.
On the Weekend
  • Attend a service and serve in a service.
  • Check in with your Team Leader upon arrival and participate in the team touchdown.
  • Make sure your area of the church is in excellent order and ready for guests 30 minutes before the start of service.
  • Dress appropriately by being neat and clean in your appearance.
  • Smile a lot!
  • Proactively initiate conversations with people you don't know and look for opportunities to serve them.
  • Share good reports to encourage your team.
Ongoing (Monthly / Quarterly)
  • Quarterly 1-on-1s with your Team Leader.
  • Attend monthly campus all-team volunteer rallies.
  • Attend all team training sessions.

Section 5

Hospitality Team Guidelines

Hospitality team members create a welcoming environment for guests and volunteers to have an amazing weekend experience. This is not by creating a space, but by building connection through hospitality! The Hospitality Team's purpose is to serve all those who enter the café, commons area and volunteer hub by making them feel welcome and at home. We have the privilege of preparing beverages and snacks as well as other hospitality elements during weekend services.

Positions

Hospitality team members will be positioned throughout the café and commons area to greet guests and assist with any hospitality needs.

  • Serve our guests with a cheerful heart and a big smile! Be on the lookout for new guests you can connect with our Connection Team or regular attendees you can initiate a conversation with to help connect with our Next Step Team.
  • Be prepared with all of the hospitality elements needed to serve our guests: cups, stirrers, creamers, sleeves, tops, etc.

Note: Each location looks a little different as far as setup, etc.

Service Elements

What to do before, during, and after service.

Preparation Before Service (1hr prior)
  • Ensure all areas are set up properly and presented with excellence (correct placement of cambros, organizers, tables, chairs, trash cans, etc.)
  • Stock any supplies needed for coffee organizers, creamer dispensers, cups, napkins, etc.
  • Prepare any beverages being served (Brew coffee and fill water dispensers)
During Service (30 mins before service starts)
  • Host! It's Game Time! 1...2...3...Go Hospitality!
  • Greet your guest with a warm smile. Be intentional and offer to pour a cup of coffee or water.
  • Engage in meaningful conversation. Introduce yourself and ask them their name.
  • Offer a warm welcome.
Replenishment Between Services
  • Check and refill/replenish each item as needed.
  • Do your best and refill when guests are not directly in front of you.
  • Tidy up the area and clean counters/tables, trash and debris.
After 2nd Service Has Started
  • Continue to refill and restock through worship.
  • After the last song ends, start breaking down the stations.
  • Leave water/cups/napkins out during 2nd service.
  • Wash all serving containers, dry them with hand towels and store them in the proper location.
  • A team member makes a checklist of inventory items needed for next week.
  • Clean workspace in the kitchen (wipe down counters, sweep, clean coffee machine, etc.).
  • Clean out the fridge, if needed.
  • Remove trash and take it to the dumpster.
Section 6

Tips for Interacting with Guests & Attenders

Before Service

Be on the lookout for first-time guests. New guests may:

  • Park in the preferred parking areas.
  • Look lost or confused.
  • Be overdressed.
  • Be holding a child(ren) and not knowing where to go.

Enthusiastically welcome guests by using phrases like:

  • "Welcome to The Life Church"
  • "We are so glad you are here with us!"

Initiate conversation with guests. Use questions like:

  • "How long have you been coming to The Life Church?"
  • "Is there anything we can help you find?"

Always be on the lookout for an opportunity to create a WOW moment:

  • Offer to buy them a drink at Press Café (you can add it to your campus's tab).
  • Offer them coffee and help fix it for them.
  • Introduce them to someone else that might have something in common with them.

Instead of pointing, we always walk with guests to their location.

After Service

Be on the lookout for guests you met before service to follow up with them. Ask them:

  • "What did you think about your experience with us today?"
  • "Did you get a chance to let us know you were here by filling out one of our info cards?"

Send guests off enthusiastically. Use phrases like:

  • "Thanks for joining us today!" or "See you next week!"

Your Leader: Hospitality Team Leader
