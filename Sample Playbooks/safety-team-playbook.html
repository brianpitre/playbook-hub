Welcome

Welcome to the Team

We're so excited to have you on the team! All of our teams are vital in making people feel welcome and comfortable at our church. The information listed here is designed to equip you to host with hospitality and excellence. Our hope is that your smile, friendly face and sincere personality can contribute to the life-giving atmosphere we desire every person to experience.

Guidelines

Safety Team Member Guidelines

The purpose of the Safety Team is to keep the flock safe through active observation while serving with a smile and providing hospitality to members and guests. We work to ensure a safe environment for the staff and the entire Church. We always engage in love and respect for one another.

Positions

There are multiple Safety Team positions at each campus. They vary by location. Positions will be assigned by the Safety Team Leader at your touchdown meeting before service. Your team leader will go over the names and locations of the positions in training.

Upon Arrival

  • Check-in and retrieve a radio. Bring your issued earpiece.
  • Each service will have a touchdown meeting to give everyone their assigned positions. After the touchdown ends, you will be dispatched to your position.
  • The radio should be set to the campus's security channel.
  • Once you have the radio and earpiece in, make a radio check with the Dispatch or Team Leader to ensure you can send and receive clearly.
  • Always identify your position when transmitting on the radio (i.e. "CF1, radio check").
  • Safety Team members act as greeters until the service begins. Once service begins, your primary role is keeping the flock safe, while serving as a greeter.

During Service

  • Conduct yourself at all times with a spirit of excellence, a smile and a heart for God.
  • Be alert and communicate anything outside of the norm to ensure a safe environment for the congregation. (We should always be cordial. However, be careful to not engage in friendly conversation that prevents you from being observant.)
  • Provide a clean and safe environment in the areas outside the church building.
  • Provide and assist with emergency/medical situations.

After Service

  • Your shift concludes when you are relieved by a replacement or Team Leader.
  • Return your radio to a charging cradle. Ensure the light is on and the radio is charging. Return your host badge or take it with you.
Awareness

Active Observation

Safety team members will constantly look for behavioral abnormalities when serving such as:

  • Heavy coat on a warm day
  • Large bags/backpacks
  • Nervous/agitated behavior
  • Frequently enters and exits the auditorium
  • Overtly friendly and asks a lot of direct questions
  • Does not appear comfortable in their environment
  • Vehicles parked in front of the church exits

If you recognize behavioral concerns or any other type of issue, use the following protocol:

  • Before you engage, radio for backup: "This is (state your position), requests assistance."
  • As you approach the person, pray for God to intervene. Ask God for the right words to say.
  • Show love to the person. Ask open-ended questions and try to evaluate their situation and state of mind.
Emergency Protocols

Special Issues

Medical

Medical personnel will provide medical treatment as required. They are responsible for assessing the medical situation and directing the Safety Team Leader on whether to contact 911. They handle a quarterly inspection of medical kits and the blow-out kit in the safe room. The Safety Team's primary responsibility is to isolate the medical event — prevent onlookers, non-medical personnel and non-family members from interfering. The Team Leader will text the Campus Pastor with a summary of the medical event.

Lost Child or Adult

Immediately notify the team leader and attempt to find the lost individual. Radio all safety team members about the lost individual's description and last known location. If unable to locate the lost individual, advise the onsite Law Enforcement Officer.

Disruptive Person

As you approach the person, pray for God to intervene. Ask God for the right words to say. Call for assistance/backup. Calmly approach the person and if necessary aid in escorting them from the premises. Whenever possible, the Law Enforcement Officer should perform the escort and will always make the call for the best next step.

Extreme/Severe Weather

Immediately notify the team leader and campus pastor. The safety team will help give directions and assist all guests to a safe room. Give instructions to meet in a designated shelter area such as the interior hallway or restroom that is away from windows.

Fire

If the fire is small, remove any individuals in the area and alert the team leader. Locate the nearest fire extinguisher and attempt to extinguish the fire. Contact the campus pastor and local fire department. In the event of a large fire, fire alarms will be activated. All safety team members will check their areas and communicate status to the team leader. If fire or smoke is present, evacuation is required — announce "evacuate the building, this is not a drill" and guide guests to evacuate quickly and safely.

Suspicious Package / Bomb Threat

Do not touch, tamper or move any items that look suspicious. Immediately notify the team leader and onsite Law Enforcement Officer. The safety team leader and campus pastor will alert law enforcement.

Safety Team Addendum

GTP

Germantown Parkway Location — Positions

*P = Priority Position   *A = Armed Position. All positions assigned by Service Team Leader at "touchdown" before service.

Service Lead *P

Responsible to the Campus Lead for ensuring the safety of the staff and guests. Touchdowns with the team 45 minutes before service, assigns positions, disseminates pertinent information and prays for the safety of all campuses and teams. Releases the Safety team at end of service.

Service Lead Alternate

In training to be the Service Lead. Maintains knowledge of each position and supports the lead as necessary.

Front Spine *P

Responsible for ensuring the kitchen and office doors are secure. Greets at front doors before service begins. Once service starts, stands by the front connection desk door to control entry to the back offices. Also responsible for keeping the area clean and free of trash.

Mid Spine *P

Responsible for the area from the kitchen to the bathrooms. Ensures sanctuary doors are secure during communion, fresh start and offering (called by Audit 1). Keeps café area free of trash. Ideally staffed with a medical provider.

Rear Spine *P

Responsible for the restroom area to the rear parking doors. May be asked to clear the men's bathroom and stand watch while children go in. Primary role is security; also serves as greeter and keeps area clean.

Spleen *P*A

Maintains physical security of the back side of the church during service. Greets people at those doors and announces when the Lead Pastor has entered the Church. Ensures no one enters the sanctuary doors during prescribed times referred to by Audit 1.

CF2 — Chair Left

Initial position is at the front doors of the auditorium as a greeter until countdown begins. Then takes a seat in the front left (looking at the stage), worshiping and praying while monitoring. Intervenes only if someone approaches the stage outside of prayer/communion time.

CF1 — Chair Right *P*A

Initial position is at the front doors of the auditorium as a greeter until countdown begins. Then takes a seat in the front right (looking at the stage). Worships and prays while monitoring. Intervenes only if someone approaches the stage outside of prayer/communion time.

CF3

Initial position is by the spleen doors greeting people. Once countdown begins, moves to seats to the right of the stage (as looking at it), in the labeled reserved seat with the black wall to the immediate right. Remains in auditorium until majority of people leave.

Audit 1 *P*A

Checks out security key and radio on arrival. Initially greets by the media booth 15 minutes before service. Once countdown begins, takes position in the back of the auditorium, maintaining line of sight with CF1 and CF2. Communicates with Front Spine and Rear Spine for communion, fresh start, prayer and offering. Maintains security of the offering as it is collected and taken to the finance room.

Audit 2 *P

Greeter at main sanctuary doors before service. During service, located in the back of the auditorium by the main doors. Steps outside to secure doors when Audit 1 makes the call. Maintains eye contact with CF positions and Audit 1.

Audit 3

Greeter at the rear sanctuary doors before service. During service, located in the back of the auditorium by the rear doors. Maintains eye contact with CF positions.

Medical Position

Ideally positioned at mid spine. May be a first responder, medic, nurse, or doctor. Responsible for assessing the medical situation and directing the Safety Lead on whether to contact 911. Handles quarterly inspection of medical kits and the blow-out kit in the safe room. If a medical emergency is announced over radio, grabs the bag and/or AED and brings it to the site.

Police Officer *P*A

GTP aims to have 3 officers at every service. Officers are priority-positioned in Front Spine, Rear Spine, Mid Spine, Auditorium, or the Spleen as needed, freeing up other safety team members.

HSL

Houston Levee Location — Positions

*P = Priority Position   *A = Armed Position. All positions assigned by Service Team Leader at "touchdown" before service.

Dispatch Responsibilities (Houston Levee)

  • Get the laptop off the shelf, plug it into the charger and log in. Set up sheet once service lead finishes.
  • The earpiece connects to analog radio but speaks through digital radio.
  • Complete attendance on the Safety team roster. Note on the sheet which officers are available.
  • Be ready for radio checks and respond promptly. Relay information to team members if they miss the touchdown.
  • During congregation's arrival, monitor all cameras on 9 or 16 views. During Praise & Worship, monitor all and check through in 4s.
  • After worship, ask for the outdoor car count (front and back) and go to Station 16 on the radio (outdoor team).
  • During altar call, the worship team goes to the green room — ask G1/G2 for a media and green room count.
  • "Buckets/green on move" means monitor office on spine 2 and maintain visual on the green until it's secure in the office.
  • Monitor Kids Life Cameras throughout service. Monitor Pastors in the bistro area after service.
  • Communication is key. Being alert to details is paramount. Notify service leads ASAP if needed.

Front Spine

Stands near the doors, greets guests arriving. Follows Active Observation. Once service begins, continues looking outward and pays close attention to anyone coming in. In emergencies, holds position and guides people to exits.

Mid Spine *P

Greets guests making their way to the auditorium or kids area. Follows Active Observation. In emergencies, guides people toward exits, working with Front Spine and host team.

Rear Spine *P*A

Stands at the rear entrance. Follows Active Observation. In emergencies, guides people toward exits. Ensures the rear door is locked at all times.

Audit Positions — Commands

Audit 1 [*P]: (1) "Countdown has started" (2) "Secure Doors for Tithes and Offerings" (3) "Buckets are moving" during offering.

Audit 2, Audit 3 [*P *A]: Observation and communication only.

CF1 *P*A

Primary responsibility: follow active observation, respond to directions from audit positions. In an emergency, protect Pastor John and Leslie and facilitate their safe removal. This is not a speaking position — do not use the radio unless there is an emergency.

CF 2, CF 3 & CF 4 *P

Assigned positions between the Audit positions and the stage. CF 4 must keep a close eye on the door leading from the auditorium to behind the stage — ensure no unauthorized people get through.

Kids Life Spine K1 *P

Primary responsibility is to keep the kids safe. Do not leave your position unless relieved or released by the service lead. Check the rear door on the first floor to make sure it is locked at all times (exit only).

Kids Life 2 — Second Floor K2

Primary responsibility is to keep the kids safe. Do not leave your position unless relieved or released by the service lead.

B1/G1 *P*A

Protects the staff in the Green Room and Pastor John while on stage. Ensures the safe room is clear and all doors are secured. This person must be an Armed Guard and always carry a weapon. When Pastor John is in the auditorium, assumes the B1 position — singular focus on the protection of Pastor John. (This position applies to every campus location.)

G2 *P*A

Ensures no unauthorized personnel enters the Green Room area, posting on the inside of the door that leads to the auditorium. Assists B1/G1 in ensuring all doors are secured.

S1 through S4

Sit in the upper stadium seats. Primary responsibility is to monitor auditorium movement and report potential threats to the stage to CF personnel. Sits in the highest possible seat to blend in with the crowd. Does not surrender the high ground to any potential threat.

Floater 1

Floats the hallways and checks on each position (excluding the Sanctuary and Green Room) to make sure the exit doors are secured. Responds immediately to all calls for assistance from other team members.

Sheriff's Deputy *P*A

Houston Levee aims to have 3 officers at every service, priority-positioned where appropriate.
