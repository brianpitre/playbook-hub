Welcome

Welcome to the Team

We're so excited to have you on the team! All of our teams are vital in making people feel welcome and comfortable at our church. The information listed here is designed to equip you to host with hospitality and excellence. Our hope is that your smile, friendly face and sincere personality can contribute to the life-giving atmosphere we desire every person to experience.

Team Standards

Expectations

Personal
  • Pursue a relationship with God strengthened by daily Bible reading and prayer.
  • Commit to attending one service and serving one service.
  • Commit to ongoing team training and actively participating in the life of the campus.
Team
  • Show up on time, with a great attitude and meet someone new.
  • Stay focused on excellence when serving.
  • Pray for your team members, leaders and pastors.
  • Be open to building genuine relationships with others on the team.
Team Goals

Wins

  • Create an excellent five-star guest experience for weekend services.
  • Have meaningful connections with your team members and guests.
Commitments

Responsibilities

During the Week
  • Read weekly Team Leader communication regarding rosters, service flow, weekend elements and upcoming events.
  • Use Planning Center Online to confirm or decline your rostered position for the weekend. Communicate with your Team Leader if your schedule changes.
  • Connect with your Team Leader weekly for updates and any information you may need to make the weekend great.
On the Weekend
  • Attend a service and serve in a service.
  • Check in with your Team Leader upon arrival and participate in the team touchdown.
  • Make sure your area of the church is in excellent order and ready for the full service run-through 30 minutes prior.
  • Dress appropriately by being neat and clean in your appearance.
  • Smile a lot!
  • Proactively initiate conversations with people you don't know and look for opportunities to serve them.
  • Share good reports to encourage your team.
Ongoing — Monthly/Quarterly
  • Quarterly 1-on-1s with your Team Leader.
  • Attend monthly campus all-team volunteer rallies.
  • Attend team trainings.

Guidelines

Venue Logistics Team Member Guidelines

Our Venue Logistics team prepares for our weekend worship services and other church gatherings by managing all behind-the-scenes logistics, room preparation and technical elements. By doing this we are creating a five-star guest experience and ensuring that each service goes smoothly.

Two Teams
Setup Team
Arrives before the service to set up all audio/visual equipment, Kids Life areas, hospitality elements, host team areas and signage, and more. Areas must be ready for the full service run-through 30 minutes before service.
Teardown Team
Arrives after the service to break down all areas. Teardown begins once most attendees have cleared out. Some overlap with Setup Team may occur between services.

Excellence and Cleanliness

Serving is a privilege. When you put on your host badge, you represent our church, our pastors and God. We want to provide a welcoming environment for guests.

  • Before the beginning of every service, all elements should be set up in a timely manner to ensure we are creating a five-star guest experience.
  • Ensure all areas are clean and excellent in appearance.
Service Elements

Service Elements We Support

Hospitality

Hospitality team members create a welcoming environment for guests and volunteers to have an amazing weekend experience — not by creating a space, but by building connections through hospitality! The Hospitality Team's purpose is to serve all those who enter the café, commons area and volunteer hub. We assist the hospitality team by setting up tables, banners and any other responsibilities that pertain to service.

Connections

The Connection Team exhibits hospitality and creates a welcoming and life-giving environment for everyone who comes, with a specific focus on providing first time guests with a first-class hosting experience. We assist the Connections team by setting up banners, tables and any other responsibilities that pertain to the service.

Kids Life

Kids Life team members are responsible for creating a welcoming and fun environment for all kids from babies to 5th grade. We assist the Kids Life team to create a space for children to experience a welcoming and friendly environment by setting up pipes and drapes to section off spaces for each class, setting up chairs, setting up tables and any other responsibilities that pertain to service.

Next Steps

Next Steps team members are positioned in strategic locations throughout the building and are actively looking for opportunities to create meaningful connections with attendees and guests each week. We assist the Next Steps team by helping set up banners, tables and any other responsibilities that pertain to service.

Outdoor Hosts

Outdoor Hosts are responsible for greeting guests by making them feel welcome and at home. They also help direct the flow of traffic while assisting guests as they enter and exit the parking lot. We help the Outdoor Host team by setting out outdoor signs, flags and any other responsibilities that pertain to service.

Ushers

Ushers have the privilege of serving everyone who enters the auditorium by making them feel welcome and at home. We help the Usher team prepare the auditorium by setting out chairs, setting up communion tables and any other responsibilities that pertain to service.

Guest Interaction

Tips for Interacting with Guests & Attenders

Before Service
  • Be on the lookout for guests and attendees arriving. This is the cue that all areas should be set up and ready for service.
After Service
  • Be on the lookout for guests and members. Once most of the attendees have cleared out the space, you can start proceeding with teardown.
