Introduction

Welcome to the 5 Star Team

Every weekend at Life Church, dozens of moving parts have to come together seamlessly — pastors, MCs, production teams, worship leaders, and volunteers all operating in sync. That alignment doesn't happen on its own. It happens because of the 5 Star.

The 5 Star exists to execute services through proactive communication, smooth transitions, and careful management of every service detail. When you do this role well, guests experience God's presence in a distraction-free environment, and the people on stage stay focused on the message instead of logistics.

This playbook is your guide. It covers who you are, what the role requires, the specific steps you need to follow every single week, and the standard you're expected to bring every time you serve. Read it carefully, refer back to it often, and own it.

Foundation

Purpose & Goal

Purpose Help execute services through proactive communication, smooth transitions, and the management of service details — so every service runs with the precision and care it deserves.
Goal Facilitate excellent services and ensure a 5-Star experience for every guest, every week.
The Why

When the 5 Star does their job, guests can experience God's presence in a distraction-free environment. The people on stage — pastors, MCs, and worship leaders — stay focused on their role without being pulled into service detail decisions.

When the 5 Star doesn't do their job, those distractions show up on stage, in the service, and in the room. This role is the connective tissue that holds the service together.
Standards

Key Attributes of a 5 Star

This role is not about filling a checklist. It demands a specific kind of person — someone who can read a room, anticipate needs, and act decisively without being asked. These are the qualities that define every strong 5 Star.

Proactive You don't wait to be told what's needed. You read the service flow, anticipate what's coming, and position yourself and your team accordingly before problems arise.
Assertive You communicate clearly and with confidence. When a change needs to be made or a team needs to be redirected, you do it — directly and without hesitation.
Detail-Oriented You know the service inside and out. Every item, every mic, every segment. Nothing is left to chance and nothing is assumed to be handled by someone else.
Decisive When things change — and they will — you make a call and move. Strong decision-making under pressure is one of the most important things you bring to this role.
Fast Thinker You process situations quickly and respond in real time. This role does not allow for long deliberation. You need to see what's happening and act in the moment.
Socially Intelligent You know how to communicate with different people — pastors, volunteers, production team — in a way that lands well and moves things forward without creating friction.
Service-Savvy You are deeply familiar with the service flow and the culture of the church. You understand what "excellent" looks like and you hold that standard for yourself and those around you.
Professional You carry yourself with composure and confidence. Your presentation, your communication, and your conduct all reflect the standard of the role and the team you serve.
The Role

Main Responsibilities

5 Star

The 5 Star is the connective tissue between the stage and production. You know the flow before anyone else does, you communicate changes before they become problems, and you make sure every person and every item is exactly where they need to be — at exactly the right time.

  • Know the service flow thoroughly to anticipate needs before they arise
  • Communicate between Pastors, MCs, and Service Production Teams throughout the service
  • Ensure MCs are ready for their segments — in position, mic on, ready to go
  • Prepare all necessary items for verbals and offering: Connection Cards, Print Pieces, Mics, Podiums, Tables, Props, Fresh Start Books, and any other needed elements
  • Ensure MC mics are powered on and screen is lit the moment you hand them off
  • Be the time keeper — track time-sensitive segments and communicate changes proactively
  • Facilitate service adjustments based on timing or technology issues
    Broadcast locations: changes of 2+ minutes must be communicated to all receiving locations
  • Communicate all adjustments and changes between services so teams are aligned
Onboarding

Training Process

Training happens during weekend services through a structured shadowing model. The timeline adjusts based on the individual's prior experience and role familiarity, but the progression does not skip steps. No one moves to an independent 5 Star position without completing the full training process and meeting with the Location Pastor or Lead Pastor Assistant.

Broadcast Location

PhaseFocusDescription
Weeks 1–2 I Do. You Watch. Shadow the Lead 5 Star through the full service. Observe every interaction, every handoff, every communication. Take notes. Ask questions after the service — not during.
Week 3 Campus Visit Go and observe at a Receiving Location. Understand what they receive from the broadcast campus and how the 5 Star role functions in a receiving context.
Week 4 You Do. I Help. Take the lead 5 Star role with the trainer alongside you. The Lead 5 Star evaluates readiness and team fit at this stage before you continue.
Weeks 5–6 You Do. I Watch. Operate fully independently. Trainer is present but hands off entirely unless an issue arises.

Receiving Location

PhaseFocusDescription
Weeks 1–2 I Do. You Watch. Shadow the Lead 5 Star through the full service. Observe how the receiving location integrates the broadcast flow with local service elements. Take notes. Ask questions after.
Week 3 You Do. I Help. Take the lead 5 Star role with the trainer alongside you. The Lead 5 Star evaluates readiness and team fit.
Weeks 4–5 You Do. I Watch. Operate fully independently. Trainer is present but hands off.
Before training is considered complete, every 5 Star must meet with the Location Pastor or Lead Pastor Assistant for a brief evaluation conversation.

Checklists

5 Star Checklist

Use this checklist every week — not just during training. Consistency is what separates good services from excellent ones.

Preservice

Before Full Service Run-Through
  • Print flows and message notes
  • Check in with production team
  • Gather all items needed for verbals and offering Connection Card, Print Pieces, Mics, Podiums, Tables, Props, Fresh Start Book
  • Set up headset and confirm mics are working
  • Wipe down podium and tables
  • Place glass and water under the 5 Star chair Receiving campus only
  • Confirm all teams are in place and ready to start on time
During Full Service Run-Through

Goal: Feel confident the first service will be excellent. Walk every segment. Catch every gap before it becomes a problem on stage.

Worship

  • Observe welcome, transitions, lighting, and sound Watch the clock and document any time discrepancies

MC Segments

  • Have all MCs ready for their segments — no MC should be scrambling
  • Fill in for any missing MC spots during run-through
  • Include contextual transitions between segments
  • Watch the clock throughout — flag any segments running long or short

Special Elements

  • Communicate and rehearse any special elements with the team

Message Segment

  • Test mics and headset
  • Go through message notes with the pastor
  • Use comms pack to communicate with production during message
  • Walk through all message segments: Vitec, Fresh Start, Close
After Full Service Run-Through
  • Communicate any updates or changes to the production team
  • Clean and vacuum backstage
  • Take out trash
  • Dust mop and vacuum the stage

Tech Touchdown

Goal: Ensure all teams are aligned on what's happening and are prepared for backup scenarios before the service begins.

Broadcast Location Tech Touchdown is only needed if changes were made after the Full Service Run-Through. If changes occurred, communicate them directly to the production team before the service.
Receiving Locations — Required Every Service Attendees: Production Team Producer, Assistant Producer, Worship Leader, Music Director, 5 Star, Sound, Lighting.

5 Star Leads the Touchdown

  • Walk through the full service flow, transitions, and all special elements
  • Share all known details with the group — no information left in your head alone
  • Open the floor for questions from the team

Example Questions to Address

  • When do keys need to go up?
  • How does the transition look between Vitec and Local?
  • What slides are going up during the message?
  • Do we need to adjust any segment times?
For Special Events, Conferences, or Prayer Rallies: Touch base with the Lead 5 Star the day before the service, and touch base with the Pastor or Executive Assistant during the run-through.

Post Service

Post Service
  • Discuss any tweaks or changes with Production, Pastors, and other teams
  • Communicate adjustments to the Lead 5 Star for the next service
  • Reset all items and leave the space better than you found it
