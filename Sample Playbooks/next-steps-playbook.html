Welcome

Welcome to the Team

We're so excited to have you on the team! All of our serve teams are vital in making people feel welcome and comfortable at our church. Please take some time to review this booklet. The information listed here is designed to equip you to host with hospitality and excellence. Our hope is that your smile, friendly face and sincere personality contribute to the life-giving atmosphere we want every person to experience at The Life Church.

We lead more effectively when we know what is expected of us. Clarifying expectations reduces stress, brings focus to action and makes teams more productive. These expectations are laid out in three key areas: Expectations, Wins and Responsibilities.

Team Standards

Expectations

Personal

Consistently seek personal growth and development:

  • Pursue a relationship with God strengthened by daily Bible reading and prayer.
  • Commit to attending one service and serving one service.
  • Commit to ongoing team training and actively participating in the life of the campus.
Team

Faithfully serve God's House and others:

  • Show up on time, with a great attitude and meet someone new.
  • Stay focused on guests when serving.
  • Pray for your team members, leaders and pastors.
  • Be open to building genuine relationships with others on the team.
Team Goals

Wins

Every person works together to create an amazing guest experience. When one team member wins, the whole team wins! By accomplishing at least one of these things each weekend, you help the team win.

  • Engage in a MEANINGFUL CONNECTION with someone new each week. This could be a first time guest or someone new to the church that you haven't met before.
  • Help guests and attendees identify and commit to take an appropriate NEXT STEP.
  • Meet 2–3 NEW PEOPLE each week. They could be new to the church or just new to you.
Commitments

Responsibilities

The responsibilities outlined below clarify the "what" and "when" of each role and when executed help create productive and healthy teams.

During the Week
  • Read weekly Team Leader communication regarding rosters, service flow, weekend elements and upcoming events.
  • Use Planning Center Online to confirm or decline your rostered position for the weekend. Communicate with your Team Leader if your schedule changes.
  • Connect with your Team Leader weekly for updates and any information you may need to make the weekend great.
  • Send follow-up communication to guests that you interacted with during the weekend service [Fresh Start and Discovery positions].
On the Weekend
  • Attend a service and serve in a service.
  • Check in with your Team Leader upon arrival and participate in the team touchdown.
  • Make sure your area of the church is in excellent order and ready for guests 30 minutes before the start of service.
  • Dress appropriately by being neat and clean in your appearance.
  • Smile a lot!
  • Proactively initiate conversations with people you don't know and look for opportunities to serve them.
  • Share good reports to encourage your team.
Ongoing — Monthly/Quarterly
  • Quarterly 1-on-1s with your Team Leader.
  • Attend monthly campus all-team volunteer rallies.
  • Attend team trainings.

Guidelines

Next Steps Team Guidelines

Next Steps team members are positioned in strategic locations throughout the building and are actively looking for opportunities to create meaningful connections with attendees and guests each week. We are the information hub for all things "Next Steps" (Fresh Start, Life Groups, Discovery, Water Baptism, Life Leadership) and are proactive to initiate conversations with guests/members about the appropriate next step for them.

Next Steps Host

Next Steps team members serving as hosts are strategically placed near the Next Steps station, throughout the commons area and in high-traffic areas. They greet guests as they arrive and walk in and out of the church.

  • Although there are designated Next Step stations, don't get stuck in those areas. Move throughout the building and into the auditorium.
  • Be on the lookout for someone you can help. Look to connect new guests with our Connection Team and help regular attendees identify their next step.
  • Be prepared with info cards or the Next Steps page on your phone to help provide or capture info and make it easy for someone to sign up.

Fresh Start

Next Steps team members serving in the Fresh Start area are positioned in the auditorium before service. They create meaningful connections through heartfelt conversation and authentic prayer in weekend services and with intentional follow-up throughout the week.

Pre-Service
  • Each team member will be assigned a section or seating zone in the auditorium to serve as a "section host".
  • Once doors are opened to the auditorium, walk around your section and initiate intentional conversations with guests/attenders that sit in those sections every week. Get to know them, offer assistance if needed and talk about the next steps our church has to offer.
  • The goal is to meet 2–3 new people each week and get people connected into a Life Group or Discovery.
During Service
  • Provide front-row leadership by sitting on the front row of the section assigned, engaging in worship and responding during the message.
  • Provide encouragement and prayer at the altar during ministry time. Men pray with men, women pray with women. If that is not possible, do not hold hands or embrace. Ask the person their name and their prayer request, then pray a short prayer of agreement in Jesus' name.
Post-Service
  • All team members rostered for Fresh Start will serve at the Next Steps station(s) after the service to give out Fresh Start books, offer prayer and provide information and guidance on the next steps to those who made a Fresh Start in service.
Step-by-Step Guide

Fresh Start Interaction

1Step Get ready
  • Make sure the Next Steps station is set up. Ensure there are Fresh Start books out and readily available, as well as pens and info cards.
  • Stand near the station and greet people as they exit, looking for people who may be approaching the station for a book.
  • Avoid engaging in conversation with other team members so that you can focus 100% on guests/attendees.
2Step Introduce yourself! Do your best to smile and make eye contact.
  • Ask their name and if they made a Fresh Start.
  • Congratulate them on their decision and celebrate with them.
3Step Ask questions to help identify the best way to serve them.
  • "Thanks for responding this week. Is this a first-time decision or have you had a relationship with God before and are rededicating your life?"
  • "What questions do you have about the decision you made that I can help answer today?"
  • Take your time depending on the response.
4Step Exchange Info. Ask them if they have completed an info card at some point in the past. Ask to get their information today so you can follow up.
  • If they have not filled out a card, use the info card and check the box "I made a Fresh Start."
  • If they have previously filled out a card, simply get their name and phone number to add to the Fresh Start summary sheet later.
  • You can also use the info card to sign them up for other Next Steps.
5Step Offer them a Fresh Start Book.
  • "I would love to give you a wonderful resource from our Pastor, John Siebeling."
  • If you don't notice a Bible in their hand, ask them if they own one. If not, encourage them to download a free Bible resource: The Bible App.
  • Let them know you will follow up during the week (Tuesday) to see how they are doing and coming along with the Fresh Start book.
6Step Talk with them about Next Steps. Focus on where they are and the best next step for them.
  • For a new person, it may be coming back to church next week. If they mention looking for a church you can point them to Discovery.
  • You can also encourage Water Baptism or getting involved in a Life Group to help them meet other people.
  • The Next Steps brochure should be included with the Fresh Start book.
7Step Offer to pray — Agree with the person in prayer for any needs they have.
  • "I would love to pray for you this week."
  • Be prepared to assist people with online Care and Support requests should the need for pastoral care arise.
Special Events

Discovery & Water Baptism

Discovery (1st Sunday of the month)

  • Assist in calling those signed up for Discovery to provide information and confirm their schedule for the upcoming class.
  • Assist in set-up/tear-down of Discovery room/space.
  • Select Next Steps team members will serve as table hosts — "host" a table of people participating in Discovery by engaging and leading conversation, answering questions and identifying each person's next step at your table.
  • Promote connections through both Life Groups and serving on a team.
  • Assist in entering data from Discovery: attendance, serve app, etc.
  • Follow up with Discovery participants a few days after graduation to ensure they are equipped to take a next step: Connected with Team Leader for First Serve, Connected to a Life Group, Fresh Start book/Resources, Vision Partners connection (when applicable).

Water Baptism (Quarterly)

  • Assist in calling those signed up for Water Baptism to confirm the upcoming baptism date, time of service and t-shirt size. Answer any additional questions they may have.
  • Assist with baptism tank and booth set up/tear down for Water Baptism service.
  • Assist in "Day Of" lobby and auditorium roles:
  • Check-In Host — welcome and check-in guests and provide a t-shirt and any other necessary items or info.
  • Floaters — assist guests and offer hospitality.
  • Lobby Host — walk guests to the restroom to change clothes or into the auditorium to the assigned seating area.
  • Restroom Host — assist guests with an open stall, clean up water spills and restock.
  • Line Host — assist guests through the line with gentle reminders.
  • Towel Attendant — assist guests with their towels before and after they get out of the tank.
  • Tank Attendant — assist guests with getting in and out of the tank as needed.
Hospitality

Tips for Interacting with Guests & Attenders

Before Service

  • Be on the lookout for first-time guests — they may park in preferred parking, look lost or confused, be overdressed, or be holding a child and not know where to go.
  • Enthusiastically welcome guests: "Welcome to The Life Church!" / "We are so glad you are here with us!"
  • Initiate conversation: "How long have you been coming to The Life Church?" / "Is there anything we can help you find?"
  • Always be on the lookout for an opportunity to create a WOW moment — offer to buy them a drink at Press Café, help them check their children into Kids Life, or introduce them to someone they might have something in common with.
  • Instead of pointing, we always walk with guests to their location.

After Service

  • Follow up with guests you met before service. Ask: "What did you think about your experience with us today?" / "Did you get a chance to let us know you were here by filling out one of our info cards?"
  • Send guests off enthusiastically: "Thanks for joining us today!" / "See you next week!"
