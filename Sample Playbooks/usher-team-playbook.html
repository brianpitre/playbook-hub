Welcome

Welcome to the Team

We're so excited to have you on the team! All of our teams are vital in making people feel welcome and comfortable at our church. The information listed here is designed to equip you to host with hospitality and excellence. Our hope is that your smile, friendly face and sincere personality can contribute to the life-giving atmosphere we desire every person to experience.

Guidelines

Usher Team Member Guidelines

Ushers have the privilege of serving everyone who enters the auditorium by making them feel welcome and at home. We help create an excellent and distraction-free environment by helping guests find seats, assisting with the offering and communion, setting out chairs, addressing service disruptions and any other responsibilities that pertain to service.

Positions

Ushers are positioned in strategic and visible locations around the auditorium. We should stand out by our actions and eagerness to serve, not because we are wearing a host badge. We do this by using positive and expressive body language, having upbeat facial expressions and smiles and offering flashlight assistance when the auditorium is dark.

Environment

Provide a Welcoming Environment

Excellence and Cleanliness

  • Before the beginning of every service, all elements should be checked, cleaned and restocked if needed. (Trash cans, tissue boxes, communion, offering buckets, info cards and offering envelopes in seat backs.)
  • Clean and reset the auditorium at the end of every service.
  • Personal Items that are left should be taken to the lost and found area after the last service.

Seating and Traffic Flow

  • Greet all guests with energy and enthusiasm. Make eye contact and smile.
  • We seat "front-to-back" — seating guests as close to the front as they are willing to go.
  • We do not force people to sit in a particular seat, but we do control the opening of closed sections. Usher Team Leads will communicate when sections are to be opened.
  • If you notice a family coming in with a small child, seat them in the reserved family section. This allows an easy exit if their child becomes fussy. Remind them about Kids Life and give them a Kids Life card.
  • Accommodate those in wheelchairs and special needs by seating them in designated areas. Keep aisles clear.
  • Main auditorium doors are closed before each service and typically open 15 minutes before service begins. Check with your Team Leader before opening any doors.
  • Where applicable, doors may be utilized to direct traffic to different areas of the auditorium. The Team Leader should make the call and communicate with Door Greeters so they can help direct guests.
  • Doors should remain closed and movement limited during prayer moments, Fresh Start and offering.

Distractions During Service

  • If you see or hear any distractions, advise your Team Lead. All distractions will be handled at their discretion.
  • A distraction is anything that takes attention away from the worship or message delivery from the platform — for example, someone playing a tambourine during worship, dancing expressively in the aisle, a baby crying, small children on a device, or someone taking a phone call.
Service Elements

Service Elements

Communion
  • Communion elements are prepared before the start of each service.
  • At the appropriate time, the elements should be placed on the tables before ministry time begins.
  • Ushers are positioned around the communion stations (if needed) to help direct the flow of traffic to the elements, prayer leaders and altar. If a long line is forming in one area, direct them to another table.
  • At locations where doors are closed during ministry time, communion elements should be offered in the lobby or appropriate area for those who are waiting to enter the auditorium.
  • Pick up communion elements when ministry time is over. They should not be left out for the duration of service.
  • Clean communion trays and reset them for the next service, if needed.
Offering
  • The person assigned to buckets ensures they are counted out for each section and are ready to be distributed. The assigned person places the buckets in each section.
  • Ushers should be prepared with Life Church pens in hand (and offering envelopes if not in seats), ready to distribute as directed by the MC or upon request.
  • At the proper time, pass one bucket down each row from left to right and front to back.
  • Collect buckets from front to back once they have been passed. Count all buckets to ensure you have received every one.
  • Take buckets to the back of the auditorium to hand to a designated Usher who will bring buckets to an area to be secured by our Safety Team.
Counting
  • Head Count: Once the message begins, count the number of people in your assigned section. Advise your Team Lead of the number counted in your section.
  • Fresh Starts: During the Fresh Start prayer, pray along in service. When the MC asks for people to raise their hands for Fresh Starts, count each raised hand in your section. Advise your Team Lead of the number counted in your section.
  • Other: On special occasions, we may also count other things such as people standing or responding to other calls to action.
Appearance

Attire

Ushers each have a personalized name badge that is checked in and out to identify you as a host team member. We want to be visible but not stand out or be distracted by our appearance so we must be thoughtful when choosing the right outfit to wear. Think business-casual.

Please avoid:

  • Loud/bright colors, including neon-colored tennis shoes
  • Sweatpants, shorts or tracksuits
  • Wireless earpiece or headphones
  • Chewing gum — Mints are available in the Usher closet
  • Texting or messaging on your phone while serving
Hospitality

Tips for Interacting with Guests & Attenders

Before Service

  • Be on the lookout for first-time guests — they may park in preferred parking, look lost or confused, be overdressed, or be holding a child and not know where to go.
  • Enthusiastically welcome guests: "Welcome to The Life Church" / "We are so glad you are here with us!"
  • Initiate conversation: "How long have you been coming to The Life Church?" / "Is there anything we can help you find?"
  • Always be on the lookout for an opportunity to create a WOW moment — offer to buy them a drink at Press Café, help them check their children into Kids Life, introduce them to someone they might have something in common with, or if you see that a tire is low, air it up for them.
  • Instead of pointing, we always walk with guests to their location.

After Service

  • Follow up with guests you met before service. Ask: "What did you think about your experience with us today?" / "Did you get a chance to let us know you were here by filling out one of our info cards?"
  • Send guests off enthusiastically: "Thanks for joining us today" / "See you next week"
